Physics Formulas

Displacement Time - Velocity - Speed - Acceleration - Two Dimensional Motion - Falling Objects - Newton’s Laws

Displacement (\( s \))

\( s = s_0 + v_0 t + \frac{1}{2} a t^2 \)

Velocity (\( v \))

Average Velocity: \( v_{avg} = \frac{\Delta s}{\Delta t} \)

Final Velocity (constant acceleration): \( v = v_0 + at \)

Speed

\( \text{Speed} = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Time}} \)

Acceleration (\( a \))

\( a = \frac{\Delta v}{\Delta t} \)

Two-Dimensional Motion

Horizontal motion: \( x = v_{0x} t \)

Vertical motion: \( y = y_0 + v_{0y} t - \frac{1}{2} g t^2 \)

Horizontal velocity: \( v_x = v_{0x} \)

Vertical velocity: \( v_y = v_{0y} - g t \)

Falling Objects

Displacement: \( s = \frac{1}{2} g t^2 \)

Final velocity: \( v = g t \)

Newton's Laws of Motion

First Law (Law of Inertia): An object remains at rest or in uniform motion unless acted upon by an external force.

Second Law: \( F = ma \)

Third Law: For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.
