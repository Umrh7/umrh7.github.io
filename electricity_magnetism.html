Electricity & Magnetism Formulas

Static Electricity - Electrical Current - Magnetism - Electromagnetic Induction

Static Electricity

Coulomb's Law: \( F = k_e \frac{|q_1 q_2|}{r^2} \)

  • \( F \) = electrostatic force
  • \( k_e \) = Coulomb's constant (\( 8.99 \times 10^9 \, \text{N m}^2/\text{C}^2 \))
  • \( q_1, q_2 \) = magnitudes of the charges
  • \( r \) = distance between the charges

Electrical Current

Ohm's Law: \( V = IR \)

  • \( V \) = voltage
  • \( I \) = current
  • \( R \) = resistance

Power: \( P = IV \)

  • \( P \) = power
  • \( I \) = current
  • \( V \) = voltage

Magnetism

Magnetic Force on a Moving Charge: \( F = qvB \sin(\theta) \)

  • \( F \) = magnetic force
  • \( q \) = charge
  • \( v \) = velocity
  • \( B \) = magnetic field
  • \( \theta \) = angle between \( v \) and \( B \)

Magnetic Force on a Current-Carrying Wire: \( F = ILB \sin(\theta) \)

  • \( F \) = magnetic force
  • \( I \) = current
  • \( L \) = length of the wire
  • \( B \) = magnetic field
  • \( \theta \) = angle between \( I \) and \( B \)

Electromagnetic Induction

Faraday's Law of Induction: \( \mathcal{E} = -N \frac{d\Phi_B}{dt} \)

  • \( \mathcal{E} \) = electromotive force (emf)
  • \( N \) = number of turns in the coil
  • \( \Phi_B \) = magnetic flux

Magnetic Flux: \( \Phi_B = B A \cos(\theta) \)

  • \( \Phi_B \) = magnetic flux
  • \( B \) = magnetic field
  • \( A \) = area
  • \( \theta \) = angle between \( B \) and the normal to the area

Induced EMF (straight conductor): \( \mathcal{E} = Blv \)

  • \( \mathcal{E} \) = electromotive force (emf)
  • \( B \) = magnetic field
  • \( l \) = length of the conductor
  • \( v \) = velocity
