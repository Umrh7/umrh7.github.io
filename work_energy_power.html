Work, Energy & Power

Work-Energy Theorem - Conservative Forces and Potential Energy - Nonconservative Forces - Conservation of Energy - Power

Work-Energy Theorem

Work-Energy Theorem: \( W = \Delta KE \)

\( \Delta KE = \frac{1}{2}mv_f^2 - \frac{1}{2}mv_i^2 \)

\( m \) = mass

\( v_f \) = final velocity

\( v_i \) = initial velocity

Conservative Forces and Potential Energy

Potential Energy (Gravitational): \( U = mgh \)

\( m \) = mass

\( g \) = acceleration due to gravity

\( h \) = height

Conservation of Mechanical Energy: \( E_{mech} = KE + PE = \text{constant} \)

\( KE \) = kinetic energy

\( PE \) = potential energy

Nonconservative Forces

Work done by Nonconservative Forces: \( W_{nc} = \Delta E_{mech} \)

\( W_{nc} \) = work done by nonconservative forces

\( \Delta E_{mech} \) = change in mechanical energy

Conservation of Energy

Conservation of Energy: \( E_{total} = KE + PE + E_{nc} = \text{constant} \)

\( E_{total} \) = total energy

\( E_{nc} \) = energy due to nonconservative forces

Power

Power: \( P = \frac{W}{t} \)

\( P \) = power

\( W \) = work

\( t \) = time

Power (Instantaneous): \( P = Fv \)

\( F \) = force

\( v \) = velocity
